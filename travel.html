The World is Diverse

The reason why I grew up in Ghana is because my parents moved there around a few years before the year 2000 (I don't really remember when). And because of this, every year I would travel back to India to visit my grandparents and other extended family members. The beautiful thing was, Ghana and India are so far away that there were no connecting flights, so we would spend a week over at a country of our choice. This is where my love for travelling began.

Some of the most breathtaking countries I have ever been to are Singapore, Turkey, Italy, and South Africa. Here are some of the unique things about these countries that I enjoyed when I visited.

Singapore, the richest island city-state.

In my opinion, one of the most impressive countries has to be Singapore. You could drive across the entire island country in just half an hour! But what makes this tiny country special is that even though it's so small, it is an extremely developed, clean, and culturally diverse nation. With a whopping 4 national languages (English, Malay, Tamil, and Mandarin) just on this little island while being incredibly developed and one of the safest countries.

One of the most unique skylines on Earth, Singapore.

Turkey, the only country that has a city on 2 continents

Turkey has been an important nation both culturally and economically. With its deep centuries-old history and amazing cuisine, it is also home to the only city in the world that lies on 2 continents: Istanbul. Istanbul has been such an important place in human civilation for centuries even before the era of the Roman Empire looking for a new Rome that settled there. This city was the lifeline in the connection between the Western civilizations (Europe) to the Eastern civilizations (Asia).

European Istanbul (bottom) and Asian Istanbul (top) split by the Bosphorus river

South Africa's natural beauty

Probably, one of the most naturally beautiful countries I've every visited has to be South Africa. The bright red warmth of the Southern African sun is, surprisingly, so unique and breathtaking. You can't go to South Africa and not go on a Safari to witness vastly unique and untamed animals in their natural habitat as you drive along. But of course, the most naturally unique city in South Africa in my opinion has to be Cape Town. A culturally diverse city near the southern-most tip of the entire continent, Cape Town's crown jewel is the iconic Table Mountain. A glorious mountain that immediately cuts off at a specific altitiude, giving the city a shine of uniqueness and having its people proud. I think if anyone wants to visit the heart of Africa, they must visit South Africa.
